New-SelfSignedCertificate -NotBefore (Get-Date) -NotAfter (Get-Date).AddYears(3) -Subject "www.cavarestobar.com" -KeyAlgorithm "RSA" -KeyLength 2048 -HashAlgorithm "SHA256" -CertStoreLocation "Cert:\CurrentUser\My" -KeyUsage KeyEncipherment -FriendlyName "HTTPS development certificate" -TextExtension @("2.5.29.19={critical}{text}","2.5.29.37={critical}{text}1.3.6.1.5.5.7.3.1","2.5.29.17={critical}{text}DNS=NS1.SITE4NOW.NET")

Run certmgr.msc
Find the certificate under Personal/Certificates. The “Issued To” field should be localhost and the “Friendly Name” should be HTTPS development certificate

Copy the certificate and paste it under Trusted Root Certification Authorities/Certificates

When Windows presents a security warning dialog to confirm you want to trust the certificate, click on “Yes”.
